Biography

Don Norman is a pioneer in the field of Human–Computer Interaction. He is the director of The Design Lab at the University of California, San Diego. He is best known for his books on design, especially "The Design of Everyday Things."

Core Design Principles
  • Affordances: Physical properties that suggest how to use an object.
  • Signifiers: Visual cues that tell you where to interact.
  • Feedback: Clear communication about the result of an action.
HCI Insights

According to Dix et al. (Human–Computer Interaction, Third Edition), HCI deals with the design and evaluation of interactive systems for human use. Don Norman’s work supports this by focusing on usability and human-centered design.

According to Interaction Design: Beyond Human–Computer Interaction (Fifth Edition), good interaction design aims to reduce user errors and cognitive load. Don Norman contributed by introducing clear design principles.

According to Don Norman, users should not be blamed for mistakes; poor design is usually the cause of user errors.

According to Wikipedia, Don Norman introduced key concepts such as:

  • Affordances – show possible actions
  • Signifiers – indicate where interaction should occur
  • Feedback – inform users about the result of actions
  • Constraints – limit incorrect actions

According to Interaction Design (Fifth Edition), these concepts help users build correct mental models and improve usability.
