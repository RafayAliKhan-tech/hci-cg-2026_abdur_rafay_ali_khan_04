Biography

Don Norman is a pioneer in the field of Human–Computer Interaction (HCI). He is best known for his influential book, "The Design of Everyday Things," where he introduced concepts like:

  • Affordances: What an object can do.
  • Signifiers: Where an action should take place.
  • Feedback: Information about the result of an action.
"Design is really an act of communication, which means having a deep understanding of the person with whom the designer is communicating."
— Don Norman
